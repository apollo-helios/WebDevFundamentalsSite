Kilkenny Castle Introduction

The Castle of Kilkenny is located in the town of Kilkenny known in Irish as Chill Chainnigh. The castle was first constructed as a wooden motte and bailey castle by Robert de Clare, otherwise known as Strongbow. It was constructed on a hill that overlooked several important trade routes and a ford across the River Nore, later it was rebuilt with stone.

When the castle was originally built it was a classic square castle with 4 round drum towers connected with curtain walls. Nowadays the castle only has three walls and three towers, because during the Cromwellian War in Ireland it was besieged by Parliamentarian forces, and the Eastern wall and North-Eastern tower were severely damaged.

Important Events in Kilkenny Castle's History

Date Event
1173 C.E. The Normans erect a wooden motte and bailey castle on the site of Kilkenny Castle
~1185 C.E. The original motte and bailey castle burns down
1195 C.E. The Earls of Pembroke start construction on the stone castle
1213 C.E. The stone castle is completed
1391 C.E. The Earls of Ormonde purchase the castle from the Earls of Pembroke
1650 C.E. Cromwell besieges the castle and destroys the eastern wall and north-eastern tower
~1700s C.E. The castle becomes run-down from neglect of the Butler family
~1850s C.E. The Earl of Ormonde begins an extensive restoration and renovation of the medieval castle
1922 C.E. The castle falls under the control of the Irish Republican forces and is besieged by the Irish Free State forces
1935 C.E. The Earl of Ormonde sells their possessions and moves to London, the castle is abandoned
1967 C.E. The Earl of Ormonde sells the castle to the Irish state for a nominal sum of £50, to become a popular tourist destination
